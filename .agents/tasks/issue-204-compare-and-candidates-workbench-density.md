# Issue #204 — Compare and Candidates Workbench Density

## Goal
Increase density and decision usefulness in `Compare` and `Candidates` so they behave like linked workstation views rather than separate lightweight surfaces.

## Why this matters
QuantLab's value is not just generating runs. It is narrowing, comparing, and deciding with explicit local state.

## Scope

### In scope
- compare hierarchy
- candidates density
- shortlist/baseline visibility
- action placement and summary blocks

### Out of scope
- new decision contracts
- portfolio semantics
- landing work

## Relevant files

- `desktop/renderer/modules/tab-renderers.js`
- `desktop/renderer/styles.css`

## Expected deliverable

Compare and candidates surfaces that feel consistent with the runs workbench and improve operator decision speed.

## Done when

- compare is visually denser and easier to rank
- shortlist and baseline state are always legible
- both surfaces feel part of one workstation grammar
